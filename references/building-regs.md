# UK Building Regulations - Key Reference for D/A Architecture Intelligence

Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/approved-documents
Merged PDF (Oct 2024): https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/6717d29438149ce9d09e3862/The_Merged_Approved_Documents_Oct24.pdf

## The Four Kickers (affect feasibility, cost, or massing)

---

### 1. Part B (Fire Safety) + Building Safety Act 2022

**Triggers:** Building height ≥18m OR ≥6 storeys

| Threshold | Requirement | Cost / NIA impact |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| ≥18m or 6F | Enhanced fire strategy, BS 9991 compliant design | ~£30-50/m² cost uplift |
| ≥30m or 10F | **Second staircase mandatory** (BSA 2022) | £1.2-1.5M cost + 280-340m² NIA lost to additional core |
| ≥50m or 16F | Full Higher Risk Building (HRB) regime - BSR oversight, enhanced structural spec, Gateway 2+3 approval | £2-3M+ cost uplift, 6-12 months programme addition |

**Key refs:**
- Approved Document B2 (Fire Safety - Buildings other than dwellings), 2022 edition
- Building Safety Act 2022, Section 65-76 (Higher Risk Buildings)
- BS 9991:2015 + 2021 amendment (fire safety in the design of residential buildings)

**In synthesis:** Always cite for schemes ≥18m. Quantify the second staircase cost explicitly at 30m+.

---

### 2. Part M (Access to and Use of Buildings) - Volume 2 (Non-dwellings)

**Triggers:** All new commercial buildings ≥2 storeys

| Floors | Lift requirement | Core m² implication |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 2-5F | 1 lift minimum (M2 §3.5) | ~8-10m² per lift |
| 6-10F | 2 lifts minimum (M2 §3.5) | ~16-20m² |
| 11F+ | 3+ lifts + fireman's lift | ~24-30m² |
| 30m+ (BSA) | Second core = second lift bank | adds 2 lifts |

**Key refs:**
- Approved Document M, Volume 2 (2015 edition, updated Oct 2024)
- M2 Section 3: Horizontal and vertical circulation
- BS 8300 (access needs for disabled people) - companion standard

**In synthesis:** Core m² calculation must account for Part M lift provision. 22% core assumption holds only if lift count is correct. At BSA 30m+ schemes, second staircase also requires a second lift bank - double check the 22% doesn't compress.

---

### 3. Part L (Conservation of Fuel and Power) - Volume 2 (New buildings other than dwellings)

**Triggers:** All new commercial buildings

| Scenario | Requirement | Cost implication |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| All commercial new build | U-values: walls ≤0.26 W/m²K, roofs ≤0.18, glazing ≤1.6 | Standard in base spec |
| GLA scheme / OA designation | BREEAM Excellent mandatory (GLA Energy SPG) | +£40-60/m² on construction cost |
| Large scheme ≥1,000m² GIA | Energy Strategy required at planning (GLA stage) | Planning condition - adds programme |
| All new commercial | Notional building target (SBEM calculation) | Fee ~£15-25k for energy consultant |

**Key refs:**
- Approved Document L2 (2021 edition, amended 2023)
- GLA Sustainable Design and Construction SPG (2014, under review)
- BREEAM UK New Construction 2018 (updated 2023)

**In synthesis:** For GLA/OA sites, BREEAM Excellent is not optional - flag as a line item cost in the appraisal context. At £50/m² on a 5,000m² NIA scheme that's £250k - not trivial.

---

### 4. Part O (Overheating)

**Triggers:** All new residential buildings (mandatory); commercial buildings where mixed-use includes resi OR where facade is highly glazed

| Situation | Requirement | Risk |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| Resi in a mixed-use scheme | Dynamic thermal modelling (simplified or detailed method) | If failed: louvres, external shading, or less glazing required = cost + facade redesign |
| Commercial with high WWR (>50%) | Not mandatory but GLA may condition it | Especially south/west elevations |
| CAZ/dense urban heat island locations | Higher baseline temperature = harder to pass | Bankside/City particularly exposed |

**Key refs:**
- Approved Document O (2021 edition)
- CIBSE TM52 (Overheating criteria for non-resi) - companion guidance
- CIBSE TM59 (Dynamic thermal modelling for dwellings)

**In synthesis:** Flag for any scheme with resi component OR where facing evidence suggests south/west glazed facade. Don't flag on pure office schemes with standard WWR - it's background noise.

## Not in synthesis (background reference only)

- **Part A (Structure):** Relevant for air rights/transfer structures - flag only when roof addition or podium-tower interface is the scheme. Transfer slab adds ~400-600mm floor-to-floor clearance loss on podium.
- **Part C:** Contamination/moisture - covered separately in site history
- **Part E:** Sound - flag for mixed-use (office/resi stacked) schemes only
- **Part F:** Ventilation - affects M&E plantroom allocation (~3-5% of GIA)
- **Part S:** EV charging - cost line if site has car parking
- **Parts G, H, P, Q, R, T:** Operational/services - not at appraisal stage
