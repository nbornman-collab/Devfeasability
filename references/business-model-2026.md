## Assumptions (all scenarios)

**Fixed costs (Year 1):**
- Railway hosting + APIs: £200/month (Mapbox, OS, Google Maps, EPC)
- Domain + email: £20/month
- PI insurance (professional indemnity): £100/month
- Accounting: £150/month
- Companies House + ICO: £50/year

**Variable costs:**
- Stripe fees: 1.4% + 20p per transaction
- API calls scale with usage: ~£0.30 per report generated
- Marketing (Year 1): £0 - relationship-driven, content only
- Marketing (Year 2+): 10-15% of revenue

**Nic's baseline:**
- Current salary: £110k (SOM Senior Design Architect)
- Target take-home Year 1: £80k minimum (accept a cut to go independent)
- Corp tax: 19% (small profits rate, first £50k at 19%)
- Employer NI on salary: ~13.8%
- Dividend vs salary split optimised (£12,570 salary + dividends typical)

**ILR timing:** May 2026. Company registration Day 1 post-ILR. First invoice possible June 2026.

## Funding & Acquisition

### Should you raise?

**Not yet. Probably not ever for equity.**

Reasons to hold:
- You have no recurring revenue yet. Raising now means selling equity cheap.
- The product works without capital. Hosting is £200/month. No hardware. No inventory.
- Bootstrapped B2B SaaS companies trade at higher multiples than funded ones (no liquidation preferences, no board, no dilution)
- You're 5-6 weeks from being able to invoice. The gap is time, not money.
- Once profitable, you can raise on YOUR terms if you want to accelerate

Reasons to consider (Year 2-3, only if):
- Click-anywhere engine needs significant engineering investment (£200-300k)
- A competitor is closing the gap and you need to outrun them
- Enterprise sales require a bigger team than revenue can support

**If you do raise:** angel round (£150-250k) from property industry angels (developers, fund managers). NOT VC. VC money comes with growth expectations that don't match a niche B2B product. Property angels bring distribution + credibility + reasonable return expectations.

### Should you sell?

**Not before Year 3. Ever.**

The panel identified acquisition as the likely exit (£5-15M trade sale). Potential acquirers:
- **CoStar Group** (owns LandInsight) - they want planning intelligence
- **Glenigan** - construction pipeline data, would add feasibility
- **Rightmove Commercial** - expanding into developer tools
- **Savills / JLL / CBRE** - prop-tech arms buying vertical tools

**When to consider selling:**
- You have 200+ paying subscribers and provable recurring revenue
- A strategic acquirer approaches (don't go looking)
- The market is consolidating and being independent becomes harder
- You've personally reached a number that changes your life

**When NOT to sell:**
- Before product-market fit is proven (you get lowballed)
- When you're growing fast (you leave money on the table)
- To a financial buyer (PE) who'll strip and flip

**Valuation benchmarks:**
- B2B SaaS at £500k ARR: 4-8x = £2-4M
- B2B SaaS at £1M ARR with growth: 6-10x = £6-10M
- Strategic premium (acquirer wants the tech): 8-15x = serious money
- Property data companies trade at premium multiples (CoStar paid 10x+ for OnTheMarket)

### The honest take

Build to £500k ARR bootstrapped. That takes 2-3 years in Scenario B. At that point you have three options:
1. Keep going (£1M+ ARR by Year 5, comfortable life, no boss)
2. Raise a small round to accelerate click-anywhere + enterprise (£150-250k for 10-15% equity)
3. Sell to a strategic acquirer for £3-5M (life-changing, not generational)

Option 1 is the default. Options 2 and 3 are decisions you make from a position of strength, not necessity.

**The worst outcome is raising money before you need it, because you give away equity when your leverage is lowest.**
