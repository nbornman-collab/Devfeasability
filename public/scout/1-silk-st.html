Heritage Setting — Hard Constraints
🏛️ Three major designations within 100m
Grade II* Listed — Largest single listed structure in the UK Barbican Estate — immediately NW. Towers, podium, arts centre, residential. The estate as a whole is the listed asset — any new building must demonstrate no harm to its setting. Historic England will object if the setting argument isn't watertight.
Grade I Listed — Medieval church St Giles-without-Cripplegate — within the Barbican precinct, NW corner. Grade I = highest heritage protection. Setting must not be obscured or dominated by new development.
LVMF Protected Views — St Paul's Cathedral Multiple view corridors run through this area. Any development above ~25m requires a verified view assessment submitted to the GLA. Barbican towers at 123m have established a tall building precedent — but they are the listed exception, not the rule.
Barbican & Cripplegate Conservation Area This site is within or immediately adjacent to the CA boundary. All development proposals require a Heritage Impact Assessment and conservation area appraisal response. Character of the area — including the relationship between new and listed buildings — is a primary consideration.

Planning Flags
🏛️
NOT in Eastern Cluster. Tall building policy support is not automatic here. Each floor above the urban grain (~8–10F) requires positive justification in heritage, design quality, and public benefit terms.
⚠️
Heritage Impact Assessment mandatory. NPPF §199–208 applies at all scenario heights. Barbican Estate Grade II* setting is the primary constraint — harm finding = statutory refusal bar.
📋
GLA referral required above 30m. Stage 1 and Stage 2 Mayor referral. Heritage arguments will be tested at both stages. Historic England likely to make representations.
🔍
Retrofit futility case needed. City planners will ask why demolition is justified over refurbishment. EPC non-compliance and structural obsolescence are the primary arguments. Must be demonstrated, not assumed.
💡
Public benefit argument is critical. At height, public realm, cultural contribution, and community uses become part of the planning case — not optional extras. The relationship with the Barbican Arts Centre is a planning asset if used well.
📐
BSA 2022 applies above 18m. All three scenarios are registered buildings. Above 50m, Higher Risk Building regime triggers — mandatory 2nd staircase, simultaneous evacuation, Gateway process.
