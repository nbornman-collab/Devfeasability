Planning Flags

🏛️
NOT in Eastern Cluster — positive case required at every floor above urban grain
City of London Local Plan designates this as Mid-City commercial area. Tall building policy (CS10/CS15) supports height where it can be justified — but doesn't grant it automatically as in the Eastern Cluster. Each scenario requires its own planning justification.
⚠️
Heritage Impact Assessment — non-negotiable, all scenarios
NPPF §199–208. Barbican Estate Grade II* is the primary constraint. "Less than substantial harm" requires public benefit outweighing. "Substantial harm" is a near-certain refusal. HIA from a qualified heritage consultant is required before any massing is fixed.
📋
Retrofit futility case must be made and evidenced
City planners will ask why demolition is justified. EPC non-compliance (2030 minimum EPC B target for commercial lets) and structural obsolescence are the arguments — but they must be evidenced in an Energy and Sustainability Assessment, not assumed.
💡
Public benefit and cultural contribution — planning asset if used well
At this scale and location, adjacent to the Barbican Arts Centre, the relationship between new development and the cultural offer is a material planning consideration. Active ground floor uses, public realm, and affordable workspace (Policy E1/E2) are expected contributions, not optional extras.
📐
BSA 2022 — registered building above 18m, Higher Risk above 50m
All three scenarios exceed 18m. Above 50m: mandatory 2nd staircase, simultaneous evacuation, Gateway 1/2/3 process, Building Assessment Certificate. The 50m threshold is a hard cost and NIA inflection point.
🌐
GLA referral — Stage 1 & 2 Mayor direction above 30m
All scenarios above 30m require GLA referral. Heritage arguments will be tested at both stages. Historic England typically makes representations on tall building proposals adjacent to Grade II* assets.
