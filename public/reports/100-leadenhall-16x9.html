05 Planning Context & Conclusions
Planning Policy Framework
  • CS1 CoLP 2040 — City of London Local Plan 2040, Policy CS1: Tall Buildings supports intensification in the Eastern Cluster
  • Site within the Eastern Cluster tall building zone — tall buildings supported in principle subject to design quality and heritage assessment
  • Leadenhall Market Grade II* — 120m NW. Townscape & Visual Impact Assessment (TVIA) required; views from and to the market to be protected
  • Lloyd's of London Grade I — 80m E. Daylight and sunlight sensitivity; BRE Guidelines assessment mandatory for consented scheme
  • BREEAM Outstanding target required for major City of London office consent (Pol ENV1); onsite renewable energy strategy expected
  • PTAL 6a — Highest public transport accessibility. Zero car parking; Class E cycle parking (1 space per 2 employees) mandatory (CoLP T4)
Design Intelligence
2.4× FAR vs cluster benchmark — site is significantly underbuilt relative to neighbouring towers; strong planning rationale for intensification
22% GIA core efficiency — market standard for Grade A City office; net:gross ratio of 80% achievable with current massing
7m+ clear lease span on minimum 2 faces achieved with proposed massing — compliant with CoLP minimum leasable floorplate requirement
Recommended Next Steps
01 Pre-application meeting with City of London Planning Department — establish heritage and massing parameters early
02 TVIA + daylight/sunlight assessment — commission specialist heritage and environmental consultants
03 RIBA Stage 1 design development — refine massing, establish core layout, and develop sustainability strategy to BREEAM Outstanding pathway
