- **Address:** Convoys Wharf, Watergate Street / Deptford Strand, London SE8 3JH
- **LAT:** 51.4856 · **LNG:** -0.0272
- **Site area:** 166,000 m² (16.6 ha / 41 acres) — single freehold

## Heritage
- **Olympia Building (Mast House):** Grade II listed. Must be retained and incorporated.
- **Site of former Royal Dockyard of Deptford (1513–1869):** Significant archaeological interest
- **Proposed conservation buffer:** Historic England will be consulted on setting

## Planning context
- Lewisham Opportunity Area (OAPF designation)
- Outline consent: outline only — all plots require Reserved Matters Applications
- Boris Johnson call-in: Lewisham initially refused; Mayor overrode
- Affordable housing controversy: community campaigned for 50% affordable; consented at ~15%
- Community opposition: Voice4Deptford campaign active; judicial review attempted
- Design quality requirement: GLA expects exemplary design at this location

## Transport
- Deptford (Overground): 12min walk
- New Cross (Overground/National Rail): 10min walk  
- Deptford Bridge (DLR): 8min walk (5min to Canary Wharf)
- No London Underground access — this is a real constraint
- Possible Bakerloo Line extension: Deptford on proposed route; no confirmed date

## Key constraints
1. Olympia Building retention mandatory (Grade II)
2. Thames Policy Area — height restrictions apply, LVMF views of St Paul's dome from Greenwich Park
3. Archaeological sensitivity — entire site is former Royal Dockyard
4. No Tube access — premium haircut vs Zone 1 sites
5. Affordable housing: public expectation vs viability gap (especially at scale)
6. Deptford Creek flood risk (Zone 3 — mitigation required)
7. Community resistance established (Voice4Deptford campaign)
