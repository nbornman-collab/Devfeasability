## T1 Copy Tone — Critical Instruction
*Confirmed by Nic Bornman, 2026-03-15*

The T1 Scout copy works when it compels a serious person to pay for T2 — not because it sells at them, but because it gives them enough to know exactly what they don't yet know.

**The test:** A smart, seasoned developer scanning T1 should finish reading and think "I need to go deeper on this" — not "this is trying to get my money."

**Rules:**
- Write like an architect who's done their homework, not a salesperson who wants a conversion
- Lead with constraints and context before GDV — seasoned people trust the analysis more when you show the obstacles first
- Never overstate the opportunity. If the heritage fight is real, say so. If the return curve peaks lower than they hope, the tool should show it
- The blurred figures in the teaser work because the reader already trusts the unblurred content. If the unblurred content is shallow, the blur trick fails
- Smart people sniff opportunism immediately. One line of hype undoes three paragraphs of credibility
- The goal: "you have no option but to pay for more if you are serious about this site" — earned, not manufactured

**The balance:**
- Enough substance to compel, not so much that T2 feels redundant
- T1 = size of the prize + primary constraints surfaced cold
- T1 does NOT explain the return curve, does NOT give margin figures, does NOT advise on which scenario to pursue
- Those three things are what you pay for
