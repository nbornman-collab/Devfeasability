## Business Model Overview

### Pricing Tiers

**T1 Scout** — Free
- Borough-level screener, lead acquisition

**T2 Appraise** — £299–499/mo
- Core paid product: massing, zoning, pro forma
- Target: land agents, SME developers, planning consultants

**T3 Present** — £499/mo+
- Polished client-ready report output
- White-label options

**T4 Commission** — Design pipeline
- Opt-in "take this further" pathway to D/A Studio
- Decoupled from tool; transparent, not embedded
- Tool knowledge creates first-call advantage

**PD Checker Pro** — £49/mo or £99/report
- Residential permitted development rights
- Separate high-volume product for homeowners, small builders, resi architects

### Revenue Streams (Priority Order)
1. SaaS subscriptions (T2/T3)
2. Design commissions (T4, decoupled, opt-in)
3. PD Checker (pro sub + one-off reports)
4. White-label / API licensing
5. Data & market intelligence reports
6. API partnerships (estate agents, mortgage platforms)

### Opening Positions

**Sarah Chen (VC):**
Real question is whether this is a standalone business or a feature that Nimbus/Aprao absorbs. TAM in London: 3,000–6,000 active professional seats. Planning consultants at £300–500/mo? Smaller than you think — most are sole traders. T4 design commissions is a conflict of interest. No commercial sales track record is the main investor concern.

**Marcus Webb (Developer — actual customer):**
Real competition is a person, not software. I pay a planning consultant £2–5k per site before committing. If your tool gives me a credible 15-min read first, that's the pitch. Accuracy is non-negotiable — one bad GIA calculation kills deals. T3 polished reports: I don't need them. PD rights: not my market (I do full planning).

**James Okafor (SaaS):**
Three customer segments, three price tolerances — serving all at once is hard. T2 at £299–499/mo requires weekly use to justify. Churn spikes when market slows. T4 design commissions should be killed or ring-fenced — when users think the tool steers toward founder's interests, NPS collapses. CAC is the number to watch. If content-led: CAC £200–400, payback 1–2 months. If sales-rep-led: CAC £2,000+, business breaks.

**Priya Sharma (Planning Consultant):**
Data quality is existential. Article 4 directions, emerging policies, conservation area appraisals — not reliably machine-readable. Need prominent disclaimers (not buried in ToS — on every output). Competitive threats: Aprao (pro forma), Nimbus (expanding), Glenigan (planning data), local authorities building their own tools. What I'd pay: £100–200/mo if it saves me 2–3 hours per site. Not £499.

- 80–150 subscribers

- Risk: not death, but stagnation

**Conditions:** No commercial hire, no CAC solution, product built but not sold

## T4 Decoupling Decision — Key Points

**The problem:** Tool can't be neutral AND fish for commissions. Professionals notice.

**The solution:** 
- Tool stands alone — no commission language in analysis
- Branding is clearly "by D/A Studio" — passive incumbency built in
- Single opt-in prompt on T3 output: "Want to explore this site further? Our team offers early-stage design appraisals."
- Not steering. Not embedded. Separate, disclosed, opt-in.

**The real edge:** When a developer calls for design work, you already know their site better than any competing architect they could approach cold.

---

## Liability Position

**Public data defense holds:** Tool uses OSM, PlanIt, EA, EPC, Land Registry data — all open government/public sources. No warranty of accuracy implied.

**Required action:** Prominent disclaimer on every report output (not buried in ToS):
> *"Based on publicly available data. For preliminary assessment only. Not professional planning, legal, or financial advice. Independent professional advice should be obtained before making any development decisions."*

**Consultant PI issue:** If a planning consultant prints T3 output and presents it as their professional advice without verification — that's their PI insurance issue, not the tool's.

## PD Checker Strand

Separate high-volume product. Different customer, different acquisition model.

**Coverage:**
- Article 4 direction check per address
- Conservation area / listed building flags
- Class A–C PD rights (extensions, lofts, outbuildings)
- Prior Approval vs Permitted distinction
- CIL liability flag

**Data already available:** Conservation areas (OSM), listed buildings, planning history (PlanIt), EPC/UPRN, borough lookup (Mapbox geocoding)

**What needs building:** Article 4 direction layer, PD rules engine (dimension logic per Class), consumer-grade UI, paywall

**Pricing:** £99/report one-off | £49/mo professional sub

**Why it works:**
- TAM: hundreds of thousands of homeowners/yr asking this question
- SEO acquisition: "can I build a rear extension without planning permission London"
- Low marginal cost once built
- Some PD users graduate to developer product (natural upsell)

**Priority:** After core developer product has paying customers. Sprint 3 or parallel lightweight MVP.
